Why go / vibe

Thailand is the gold standard Southeast Asia first-timer destination — absurdly photogenic temples, crystalline Andaman waters, and a street-food culture that punches above Michelin. It handles first-time international Indian travellers with ease: Hindi menus in tourist zones, zero visa hassle, and a price point that still feels like value even after the post-pandemic surge.

Top sights & places within

  • Grand Palace and Wat Phra Kaew, Bangkok — gilded complex that anchors every itinerary
  • Wat Pho, Bangkok — reclining Buddha 46 m long; oldest and largest temple in the city
  • Ayutthaya Historical Park — UNESCO royal capital ruins 1.5h north, photogenic at golden hour
  • Doi Suthep temple, Chiang Mai — hilltop golden chedi overlooking the entire northern valley
  • Phi Phi Islands (Ko Phi Phi Don and Leh) — turquoise lagoons and Maya Bay from The Beach
  • Railay Beach, Krabi — car-free limestone peninsula accessible only by longtail boat

Activities menu

  • Island-hopping and snorkeling by speedboat around Andaman archipelago (Phi Phi, 4 Islands, Phang Nga)
  • Half-day at an ethical elephant sanctuary (Elephant Nature Park, Chiang Mai) — bathing, no riding
  • Bangkok food walk: Yaowarat (Chinatown), Ari, and a Michelin-starred street stall dinner
  • Chao Phraya river and klong (canal) cruise by longtail — temple-hop without queuing on foot
  • Thai cooking class (half-day) in Bangkok or Chiang Mai: market sourcing plus 4-course cook
  • Muay Thai evening at Lumpinee or Rajadamnern stadium, Bangkok — ringside seats available

Where to stay

Bangkok: Sukhumvit (BTS-connected, shopping, nightlife) or Old City (temples at doorstep); budget guesthouses ₹1,800–3,500/night, comfort 4-star ₹5,000–10,000, luxury on Chao Phraya ₹18,000–40,000. Phuket: Patong for energy, Karon/Kata for families, Kamala/Surin for luxury quietude; comfort resorts ₹7,000–15,000, five-star pool villas ₹25,000–70,000. Chiang Mai: Old City boutique guesthouses ₹2,500–5,000 or Nimman area design hotels ₹5,000–10,000; luxury teak pool lodges ₹15,000+.

Day trips & nearby pairings

  • Damnoen Saduak or Amphawa Floating Market — 1.5h drive from Bangkok; best at 7–9am before tour buses
  • Kanchanaburi and Bridge on the River Kwai — 2h drive; WWII Death Railway, waterfall, quiet guesthouses
  • James Bond Island (Phang Nga Bay) — 1.5h by speedboat from Phuket; limestone sea caves + kayaking
  • Krabi and Railay Beach — 1.5h by ferry from Phuket; rock-climbing, four islands, no cars

What to pack

Lightweight breathable cotton (temple cover-ups or sarong essential); reef-safe sunscreen; insect repellent with DEET; stomach rehydration sachets; Grab app installed before departure; power bank for all-day sightseeing.

Getting there

  • Air: Direct 3–3.5h from Delhi or Mumbai to Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (IndiGo, Air India, Thai Airways, AirAsia); select direct Delhi–Phuket flights ~4.5h; internal Bangkok–Phuket or Bangkok–Chiang Mai ~1–1.25h on AirAsia/Thai Smile (₹3,000–7,000 booked early)
  • Rail: No rail link from India to Thailand; within Bangkok BTS Skytrain and MRT metro cover all tourist zones (single ride ₹30–80); Ayutthaya reachable by Thai Railways from Hua Lamphong station in 1.5h (₹150)
  • Road: No practical overland route from India; within Thailand public minivans connect Bangkok–Pattaya 1.5h (₹200), Bangkok–Kanchanaburi 2h (₹250); inter-city coaches Bangkok–Chiang Mai overnight 9h (₹600)

Permits / visa

The Thai Cabinet voted on May 19, 2026 to remove Indian passport holders from the visa-exemption list and reclassify them as Visa on Arrival (VOA); the change takes effect 15 days after publication in the Royal Gazette, which had not been confirmed as of late June 2026 — reconfirm the gazette effective date before ticketing. Once in effect, VOA is available at Suvarnabhumi, Phuket, Don Mueang, and select other international ports: the fee is THB 2,000 (~Rs 5,900) payable in Thai Baht cash only at the counter, the maximum stay is 15 days with no extension permitted, and travelers must hold a return or onward ticket departing within those 15 days; proof of funds required is 10,000 THB per person (~Rs 30,000) or 20,000 THB per family. For stays beyond 15 days, a Tourist e-Visa must be applied for at thaievisa.go.th before departure from India: processing takes approximately 14 working days, a single-entry visa costs ~Rs 3,000, and a 6-month multiple-entry costs ~Rs 13,500, both allowing a 60-day stay. The Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC) has been mandatory for all foreign arrivals since May 1, 2025: complete it free at tdac.immigration.go.th within 72 hours of scheduled arrival, which generates a QR code scanned by immigration at the port; airlines can and do deny boarding if the traveler cannot produce TDAC confirmation, and it fully replaces the old paper TM6 form. Passport must be valid for at least 6 months. No inner-line or protected-area permits are required for the standard tourist circuit; Chiang Rai border-zone and hill-tribe villages near the Myanmar border may involve checkpoint registration, which organised tours handle automatically.

  • West coast (Phuket, Krabi) is effectively off-season May–Oct due to monsoon, but the east coast (Koh Samui, Koh Phangan) runs its own monsoon Nov–Jan — booking the wrong coast in the wrong month lands a client on a flooded beach.
  • Songkran (mid-April Thai New Year) turns Bangkok into a citywide water fight: hotels charge 2–3× standard rates, the Grand Palace and Wat Pho are dangerously crowded, and city streets are largely impassable — price it accurately or route clients elsewhere.
  • All temples enforce the shoulders-and-knees dress code without exception; sarong rental is available at Grand Palace gates but adds roughly 25 minutes to the entry queue, particularly brutal in April heat — brief clients before they leave home.
  • At Suvarnabhumi, unofficial touts charge Rs 2,000–3,500 for fixed-price rides to the city; official metered taxis in the queued lane outside arrivals cost Rs 600–900 to central Bangkok — advise clients to pre-install Grab and use it from the airport.
  • Tuk-tuk drivers near Khao San Road routinely tell tourists the Grand Palace is closed that day and offer to take them to a gem shop instead — the palace is almost never actually closed; clients should ignore the claim and walk directly to the entrance.

Why go / vibe

Bali is Indonesia's only Hindu-majority island, producing an unbroken sensory blend of incense-scented temple courtyards, terraced paddy fields, volcanic crater lakes, and Indian Ocean surf that no other island destination replicates. It sells equally well as a honeymoon, a wellness retreat, or a young-group beach-party week — often all three in one trip.

Top sights & places within

  • Tanah Lot Sea Temple (offshore rock shrine, sunset icon)
  • Tegallalang Rice Terraces, Ubud (UNESCO-nominated subak irrigation)
  • Uluwatu Cliff Temple (70-m sea cliff, kecak fire dance arena)
  • Mount Batur active volcano & crater lake (1,717 m, sunrise trek)
  • Tirta Empul Holy Spring Temple (ritual purification bathing pools)
  • Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary, Ubud (ancient temples, 700+ macaques)

Activities menu

  • Sunrise trek to Mount Batur crater rim — 4-hr round trip, guides mandatory
  • White-water rafting on Ayung River, Ubud (Grade II–III, beginner-friendly)
  • Balinese cooking class (wet-market tour to 5-dish meal) in Ubud
  • Surf lesson on Kuta or Seminyak beach (2-hr beginner session ~USD 25)
  • Kecak fire dance at Uluwatu — 6 pm performance, book afternoon cliff walk combo
  • Traditional Balinese four-hand massage and floral-bath ritual at a Ubud spa

Where to stay

Seminyak/Legian: nightlife, beach clubs, boutique villas — budget ₹3,000/night to luxury ₹18,000/night. Ubud: cultural heart, rice-field infinity-pool villas, wellness retreats — mid ₹4,000 to luxury ₹25,000. Jimbaran/Nusa Dua: 5-star resort corridor, calm beaches, best for families. Canggu: surfer-digital-nomad zone, mid-range cafes, artsy crowd.

Day trips & nearby pairings

  • Nusa Penida (45-min speedboat from Sanur piers): Kelingking cliff, Angel's Billabong, manta-ray snorkel — full day, book boat + guide combo ~USD 35 pp
  • Gili Trawangan (2.5-hr fast boat from Padang Bai): white-sand atoll, no motorized vehicles, snorkeling — ideal overnight add-on
  • Lovina Beach, North Bali (2.5 hrs by road): sunrise dolphin-watching boat, black-sand beach, Banjar hot springs
  • Lombok (20-min flight or 2.5-hr fast ferry): quieter beaches, Sasak villages, Rinjani base — good 1-night extension

What to pack

Reef-safe sunscreen (mandatory in marine parks), sarong + sash for temples (provided free at most entrances but bring your own), DEET insect repellent, waterproof sandals + lace-up trekking shoes for Batur, universal adapter (Bali uses C/F sockets), USD cash for VoA at airport.

Getting there

  • Air: IndiGo, Air India, Batik Air fly direct Mumbai/Delhi to Denpasar (DPS) — 5.5 hrs from South India, 6.5–7 hrs from North India; fares ₹18,000–38,000 return. One-stop via Singapore (SIN) or Kuala Lumpur (KUL) adds 2–3 hrs but can be cheaper off-peak.
  • Rail: Not applicable — Bali is an island; no rail link from India or within Bali.
  • Road: Not applicable — island destination; accessible only by air or (from Java/Lombok) short ferry crossing.

Permits / visa

Indian passport holders enter on Visa on Arrival (VoA) at Ngurah Rai International Airport; the fee is IDR 500,000 (~USD 35 / ~₹3,000), payable by card or foreign-currency cash (USD, EUR, AUD, GBP, SGD accepted; INR is not accepted). A separate mandatory Bali Tourism Levy of IDR 150,000 (~USD 10), in force since 14 February 2024, applies to all foreign visitors and is cashless only — pay online before departure at lovebali.baliprov.go.id, which issues a QR-code voucher by email; total entry cost is therefore ~USD 45. Alternatively, apply for an e-VoA before departure at evisa.imigrasi.go.id or the VFS Global Indonesia portal (same IDR 500,000 fee, 30-day validity, email approval within 24–72 hours), which allows clients to bypass the airport VoA queue entirely. Both VoA and e-VoA permit a 30-day stay, extendable once for a further 30 days (IDR 500,000 / ~USD 35) at an Indonesian immigration office in person; since June 2025, all extensions require biometric collection (fingerprints and photo) under Circular Letter IMI-417.GR.01.01/2025 — online renewal is no longer accepted. Sarong and sash are mandatory at all temples and are supplied free at most entrances (occasionally charged a ₹50–100 equivalent). Nyepi Day 2026 falls 19 March 6:00 AM to 20 March 6:00 AM: Ngurah Rai Airport closes completely for 24 hours and all outdoor movement is prohibited island-wide — the date shifts each year, so reconfirm before quoting any March itinerary.

Why go / vibe

Vietnam delivers epic visual range — karst bays, terraced highlands, imperial citadels, colonial beach towns — compressed into a flyable 1,650 km strip. Street food culture rivals any on earth, and mid-range hospitality punches well above its price tag for Indian travellers on 6-10 day breaks.

Top sights & places within

  • Halong Bay — 1,600 limestone karsts rising from emerald water
  • Hoi An Ancient Town — UNESCO lantern-lit trading port
  • Hue Imperial Citadel and royal tombs
  • Phong Nha-Ke Bang caves (Son Doong / Paradise Cave)
  • Sa Pa rice terraces and Fansipan summit
  • Ho Chi Minh City — War Remnants Museum, Ben Thanh, cu chi tunnels

Activities menu

  • 2-night Halong Bay cruise — kayaking sea caves, floating villages, sunrise on deck
  • Ha Giang motorbike loop — 350 km mountain circuit through Dong Van karst plateau
  • Hoi An cooking class — market tour, clay pot fish, fresh spring rolls
  • Phong Nha cave kayak through Paradise or Dark Cave
  • Fansipan cable-car + short summit trek (3,143 m, takes 1 day)
  • Grab-bike street food crawl in Hanoi Old Quarter — bun cha, banh mi, egg coffee

Getting there

  • Air: IndiGo / Vietnam Airlines Delhi→Hanoi ~5 h direct (daily); Mumbai→HCMC ~5.5 h via Singapore or Bangkok (Vietnam Airlines, Vistara codeshare, Air Asia); return fares ₹18,000–38,000. Permits / visa

Indian passport holders require a Vietnamese e-visa; no visa-free entry or visa on arrival is available. Apply at evisa.gov.vn (the current official portal since 11 November 2024); processing takes 3–5 business days, so apply before departure. Single-entry costs US $25 (~₹2,100); multiple-entry costs US $50 (~₹4,200); the visa is valid for up to 90 days. Standard routes (Hanoi, Halong Bay, Hoi An, HCMC) require no additional permits. The Ha Giang Loop sits in a border zone: all foreign visitors must collect a border-area travel permit ("Giấy phép vào khu vực biên giới") at the immigration/police office in Ha Giang City before setting off — not at a roadside checkpoint — at approximately VND 200,000–250,000 (~US $10 pp, same-day issuance on passport presentation); self-riders additionally need a valid home-country motorcycle licence and a 1968-convention International Driving Permit (IDP), as enforcement is now active and fines are significant. Son Doong cave is operated exclusively by Oxalis Adventure under an annual quota of 1,000 visitors; as of mid-2026 all tours are fully booked through 2027, making the realistic lead time 18 months to 2+ years ahead; price is approximately US $3,000 pp (VND 79,500,000) — reconfirm availability and open booking windows before quoting clients.

⚠ Catches — reconfirm before quoting
  • Hoi An and Hue flood severely October–November from typhoon runoff; book only with travel insurance and fully flexible/refundable hotels in that window.
  • Halong Bay has a serious quality gap: boats under US $80/night carry poor safety records; require mid-tier licensed operators such as Paradise, Indochina Junk, or Heritage Line.

Permits / visa

Indian passport holders need no visa for Bhutan. A valid Indian passport with at least 6 months remaining validity, or an original Voter ID card issued by the Election Commission of India, is accepted at Paro airport and all land border crossings (Phuentsholing, Gelephu, Samdrup Jongkhar, Samtse); Aadhaar cards are not accepted at any entry point. A free Entry Permit is issued on arrival at Paro airport immigration or the relevant land border Immigration Office; online pre-application is available at immi.gov.bt and requires 4–5 working days. The Entry Permit covers Thimphu and Paro districts only — travel to any other district (Punakha, Haa Valley, Bumthang, Phobjikha, eastern districts) requires a separate Route Permit, also free, collected in person at the Department of Immigration office in Thimphu on a working day (Monday–Friday, 9am–5pm; processing takes approximately 1.5–2 hours); a driver or guide may collect it on the client's behalf, but it is issued only at the Thimphu DoI office, not at checkposts. No licensed Bhutanese tour operator is required for Indian nationals. A Sustainable Development Fee (SDF) of INR 1,200 per adult per night (INR 600 for ages 6–12; free under 5) applies and is confirmed fixed until 31 August 2027; a 5% Bhutan GST on hotel stays, transport hire, and guide fees has been in effect since 1 January 2026, replacing the previous 10% accommodation-only sales tax. Getting there

  • Air: Fly Delhi/Mumbai → Hanoi Noi Bai (HAN): 4–5 hr direct (IndiGo, Air India, Vietnam Airlines); Hanoi is the only practical air entry. From Hanoi, overnight sleeper bus to Ha Giang is 6–7 hr (departs ~9 PM, arrives ~4–5 AM; ₹600–900).
  • Rail: No direct rail to Ha Giang. Hanoi → Tuyen Quang by train (3 hr) then shared van 2 hr north is an option but slower than the bus. Use Hanoi as the rail hub for broader Vietnam legs; road-transfer onward to Ha Giang.
  • Road: Ha Giang is 300 km north of Hanoi via QL2 highway; 6–7 hr by hired car/jeep (₹5,000–7,000 one-way) or 7–8 hr overnight sleeping bus. The loop itself is ~350 km and takes 3–5 riding days.

Permits / visa

Indian nationals require a Vietnam e-Visa before arrival; apply at evisa.gov.vn (the old portal evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn was deprecated November 2024). Fee is USD 25 single entry or USD 50 multiple entry, both valid up to 90 days; allow 5–7 working days during peak season rather than the nominal 3. A separate border-zone travel permit is mandatory for all four northern districts on the loop — Quan Ba, Yen Minh, Dong Van, and Meo Vac (Lung Cu, the northernmost flagpole, falls within Dong Van district) — and must be in hand before departing Ha Giang city, since checkpoints begin at Quan Ba on Day 1. Obtain it at the Immigration/Police Office in Ha Giang city (commonly 346 Nguyen Trai St); licensed operators and guesthouses routinely process it on your behalf the same morning, but it cannot be arranged en route. Current cost is approximately 200,000–250,000 VND (~USD 10, ≈₹830) per person; enforcement has tightened since June 2026 — carry your passport at all checkpoints on the loop. No permit is needed for Ha Giang city itself; monsoon-season road closures (Jun–Aug) are administrative, not permit-related — reconfirm conditions with local operators before departure.

⚠ Catches — reconfirm before quoting
  • The border-zone permit is required from Day 1 and covers all four districts (Quan Ba, Yen Minh, Dong Van, Meo Vac); get it at Ha Giang city's Immigration/Police Office before departing — cost is ~200,000–250,000 VND (~USD 10, ≈₹830) per person, and enforcement has actively tightened since June 2026.
  • Apply for your Vietnam e-Visa at evisa.gov.vn (not the deprecated evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn); single entry costs USD 25, multiple entry USD 50, and during peak season allow 5–7 working days rather than the nominal 3 — carry a printed copy of the approval to show at the airport and the Ha Giang permit desk.
  • Monsoon season (Jun–Aug) brings landslides that close single-lane cliff roads for 1–4 days at a time with no alternate exit, collapsing itineraries and making refunds difficult to recover.
  • There are no ATMs anywhere on the loop beyond Ha Giang city — carry 3–5 days of VND cash, budgeting approximately 600,000–900,000 VND per person per day all-in.
  • Motorbike accidents are the leading cause of tourist hospitalisation in Ha Giang; self-riding demands real mountain-road experience — if in doubt, hire a licensed Easy Rider guide (₹2,500–3,500/day) or book a jeep tour.
  • Mobile signal is absent for 60–80 km stretches between Yen Minh and Meo Vac — download offline maps (OsmAnd or Maps.me), share a live GPS pin with someone before departing, and pre-book guesthouses since walk-ins fill up in October.
