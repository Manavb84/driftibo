What's inside

Chapter 1

Get inventory

You don't need signed hotel contracts to start selling trips. This chapter explains the two ready-made pipelines — bedbanks and DMCs — which providers you can realistically join as a new agency, and where to find offbeat properties that the big platforms haven't catalogued yet. Includes the step-by-step journey of how a real inventory deal moves from supplier to you to traveller.

Chapter 2

Move people

Getting people there and back is as important as the stay. This chapter covers how to access cheaper airfares through consolidators, how to sell a helicopter or charter flight without putting up any capital (through a broker who takes the risk), and which ground-transport options — shared jeeps, private transfers, trains — to stitch into your packages.

Chapter 3

Build & price a trip + documents

A package is just a bundle of inventory with a single price and a clear margin built in. This chapter shows you how to assemble the pieces, how to price with a worked rupee example so you can see exactly where the money goes, and which documents — booking confirmations, vouchers, itineraries — you hand to the traveller and to each supplier.

Chapter 4

Legal, tax & permits

Operating legally isn't complicated but it has a specific sequence. This chapter walks through the registrations you actually need (and the ones that are optional at the start), explains GST and TCS in plain words so you can file correctly, and covers the travel permits required for restricted areas like Spiti, Ladakh and the Northeast.

Chapter 5

How agents make money

Margin in travel comes from more places than most founders expect. This chapter maps every source of income — commissions, markups, B2B referrals, ancillary fees — explains the levers you can pull to grow that margin over time, and covers the risk-and-trust layer: how to hold customer money safely, what cancellation slabs look like, and how to protect the business from chargebacks.

Chapter 6

Go international

When you're ready to sell trips outside India, the supply chain shifts but the logic stays the same. This chapter covers which bedbanks and DMC networks serve international destinations, the starter countries that are easiest to enter, what compliance changes (currency rules, TCS on outbound payments), and how to offer visa assistance as a service without becoming a visa agent.

Chapter 7

The $1M plan + first 90 days

The final chapter is concrete and time-bound. It lays out the organic path to one million dollars in bookings — the math behind it, the milestones, the sequence — and then collapses that into a 90-day starting checklist so you know exactly what to do in your first three months: which registrations to file, which suppliers to approach, and which channels to activate first.
