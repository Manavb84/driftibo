Why go / vibe

Goa delivers a rare fusion of 450-year-old Portuguese colonial architecture, beaches ranging from party-loud (Baga) to utterly secluded (Agonda), and one of India's most relaxed eating-and-drinking cultures. It is small enough to cover thoroughly in a week yet layered enough that beach lovers, heritage buffs, and nightlife seekers never overlap unless they choose to.

Top sights & places within

  • Basilica of Bom Jesus and Se Cathedral, Old Goa (UNESCO World Heritage complex)
  • Fort Aguada, Sinquerim — 17th-century Portuguese sea fort with lighthouse
  • Dudhsagar Falls, Mollem — four-tiered 310-m waterfall deep inside Bhagwan Mahavir Sanctuary
  • Chapora Fort, Vagator — clifftop ruin with panoramic Vagator Beach views
  • Palolem Beach, South Goa — crescent bay with calm waters and hammock shacks
  • Fontainhas Latin Quarter, Panaji — pastel-tiled Portuguese neighbourhood with heritage cafes

Activities menu

  • Beach-hopping between North Goa (Baga, Anjuna, Vagator) and South Goa (Palolem, Agonda, Benaulim)
  • Water sports at Baga and Calangute — jet-ski, parasailing, banana boat, kayaking
  • Scuba diving and snorkelling day-trip to Grande Island (15 km offshore, visibility 5-15 m)
  • Mandovi River sunset cruise with live Konkani music and open bar
  • Full-day spice plantation tour in Ponda with Goan lunch
  • Night market circuit — Ingo's Saturday Night Market (Arpora) or Mackie's Saturday Night Bazaar

Where to stay

North Goa (Calangute–Baga–Anjuna corridor): widest choice; budget beach huts Rs 800–2,000/night, comfort hotels Rs 3,500–7,000, boutique/luxury resorts Rs 8,000–25,000. South Goa (Palolem, Agonda, Benaulim): quieter and greener; eco-huts Rs 1,200–3,000, upscale resorts Rs 6,000–20,000. Panaji/Fontainhas: heritage guesthouses Rs 2,500–5,000 for a Portuguese-quarter feel closest to ferry and heritage sights.

Day trips & nearby pairings

  • Dudhsagar Falls, Mollem (75 km, ~2.5 hrs) — full-day jeep safari; forest dept mandatory, book ahead
  • Gokarna, Karnataka (130 km, ~3 hrs) — quieter beaches, Om Beach, Mahabaleshwar Temple
  • Dandeli Wildlife Sanctuary (120 km, ~3 hrs) — white-water rafting on Kali River, jungle safari
  • Hampi, Karnataka (350 km, ~7 hrs) — UNESCO boulder-strewn ruins; best as an overnight extension

What to pack

Light cottons and linen, reef-safe sunscreen SPF 50+, reef shoes for rocky beaches, dry bag or waterproof pouch, mosquito repellent (DEET 20%+), one smart-casual outfit for nicer restaurants and casinos.

Getting there

  • Air: Goa International Airport Dabolim (GOI) or new Mopa (GOX, North Goa); ~1.5 hrs from Mumbai, ~2.5 hrs from Delhi; taxi to North Goa 45-60 min (Rs 800-1,200), South Goa 25-35 min (Rs 600-900)
  • Rail: Madgaon (Margao) station on Konkan Railway is the main hub; Rajdhani/Jan Shatabdi from Mumbai CSMT ~8-9 hrs; from Delhi ~24 hrs via LTT/Rajdhani; Thivim station (North Goa) also on Konkan line
  • Road: Mumbai to Goa ~10-12 hrs (560 km) via NH66; Pune ~8-9 hrs (450 km) via NH48 then NH66; KSRTC/private Volvo sleeper buses from Mumbai, Pune, Bangalore widely available

Permits / visa

Indian nationals need no permits anywhere in Goa; no restricted-area or inner-line permits apply. Foreign nationals require a valid Indian visa (e-Visa accepted for most nationalities). At Dudhsagar Falls, private vehicles are barred inside Bhagwan Mahavir Sanctuary; visitors must use a mandatory forest-department jeep departing from Collem/Kulem station — the railway-station trailhead, not Molem town (the national-park visitor entrance ~5 km away). Jeeps are booked at the DTOA (Dudhsagar Tour Operators Association) counter at Collem or via the GTDC online portal; a GTDC booking counter was added in November 2024 after an operator dispute, and both channels are currently active. Walking to the falls along the railway track is permanently banned under Indian Railways trespass rules. Casino entry requires guests to be aged 21 or over and to present a government photo ID.

⚠ Catches — reconfirm before quoting
  • Christmas–NYE surge (Dec 24–Jan 2): hotel rates triple and beaches are severely overcrowded — book 3+ months ahead or avoid the window entirely.
  • Monsoon (Jun–Sep) closes virtually all beach shacks, water-sports operators, and beach-facing restaurants; travel insurance is essential when booking near shoulder dates.
  • Dudhsagar Falls jeeps depart from Collem/Kulem station, not Molem town (the national-park entrance ~5 km away); slots fill on weekends and public holidays throughout the full Oct–May season; walking to the falls along the railway track is permanently banned — offenders face fines and forcible removal.
  • The forest-jeep fare at Collem is approximately Rs 400–700 per person sharing (up to 7 passengers) or Rs 2,400–3,500 for a full-jeep charter; the Rs 2,000–2,500 per-person figure quoted by many North Goa hotels is a bundled day-tour package (coach transfer + spice plantation + lunch) and must not be presented as the standalone jeep fare.
  • Scooter riders must hold a valid two-wheeler licence; since March 2024 Goa Police also require renters to sign a mandatory traffic-law undertaking; third-party insurance is frequently invalid for tourists — verify coverage before riding.
  • Drug possession under India's NDPS Act carries a mandatory minimum 6-month imprisonment; touts are persistent at Anjuna, Arambol, and Vagator beaches.

Why go / vibe

The Kerala Backwaters offer a rare slow-travel rhythm — village life, paddy fields, toddy shops, and colonial-era canals visible only by water, untouched by roads. It pairs natural beauty with genuine cultural immersion: fishermen casting nets at dawn, coir weavers at work, and sunsets melting over Vembanad Lake.

Top sights & places within

  • Alappuzha (Alleppey) — houseboat capital, 19th-century canal network and Nehru Trophy race venue
  • Vembanad Lake — Kerala's largest wetland lake, 200 sq km, heart of the backwater circuit
  • Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary — 14-acre heronry on lake shore; open-billed storks, egrets, kingfishers
  • Kuttanad — world's lowest-altitude farming zone, paddy fields below sea level visible from the water
  • Punnamada Lake — calm arm of Vembanad, quieter houseboat mooring away from Alleppey town
  • Munroe Island (near Kollam) — eight tiny islands linked by narrow tidal canals; best by punt canoe

Activities menu

  • Overnight kettuvallam houseboat cruise with Kerala dinner cooked on board
  • Dawn canoe or kayak through narrow Kuttanad village canals (inaccessible to houseboats)
  • Birdwatching walk at Kumarakom Sanctuary at 6 am (peak hour for sightings)
  • Village walk — coir rope-making, toddy tapping, and rice-paddy harvest (Oct-Feb)
  • Ayurvedic Panchakarma or Abhyanga oil massage at a lakeside resort
  • Nehru Trophy Snake Boat Race spectating (second Saturday of August, Punnamada Lake)

Where to stay

Alleppey (Alappuzha): budget guesthouses ₹900-1,800/night; comfort hotels ₹3,500-7,000; boutique canal-side ₹8,000-14,000. On-water: budget certified houseboat ₹9,000-12,000/night (boat for two, all meals); premium ₹16,000-25,000; luxury ₹30,000+. Day trips & nearby pairings

  • Fort Kochi (1.5 hrs road): Chinese fishing nets, Mattancherry spice warehouses, Dutch Palace — half-day heritage loop
  • Marari Beach (30 mins south of Alleppey): quiet fishing village beach, no crowds, good for a morning swim
  • Ambalapuzha Sri Krishna Temple (15 mins): famous palpayasam (rice-milk pudding) prasad, strict dress code
  • Thekkady / Periyar Tiger Reserve (3.5 hrs from Kottayam): bamboo-raft wildlife safari on Periyar Lake — easy add-on before or after backwaters

What to pack

Lightweight cotton/linen (humidity is relentless), DEET 30%+ repellent (mosquitoes peak at dusk on the water), compact rain jacket year-round, slip-on sandals for wet boat decks, modest cover-up for temple visits, dry bag for phone.

Getting there

  • Air: Cochin International Airport (COK) is the main gateway — ~85 km / 1.5-2 hrs to Alleppey by road; Trivandrum (TRV) serves the Kollam end, ~155 km / 3 hrs to Alleppey
  • Rail: Alleppey (Alappuzha) station is on the Kochi–Trivandrum mainline; Ernakulam Junction (Kochi) is the major hub, 1.5 hrs by train; frequent Janshatabdi/Kerala Express from both ends
  • Road: NH66 coastal highway from Kochi to Alleppey is 85 km, ~2 hrs by taxi (₹1,800-2,200); Trivandrum to Alleppey ~155 km, 3.5-4 hrs; KSRTC buses run hourly from Kochi KSRTC stand

Permits / visa

No permits are required for Indian citizens; no inner-line or restricted-area permit applies anywhere in the Kerala Backwaters. Temple dress codes at major shrines are strict: men must wear dhoti/mundu only — full-length trousers are explicitly barred at Padmanabhaswamy Temple (Trivandrum) and Guruvayur, and some temples additionally require men to be bare-chested. Women must wear a saree or mundum-neriyathum at the strictest shrines including Padmanabhaswamy; salwar kameez is accepted at many but not all major temples; shorts and sleeveless tops are barred across all major shrines. Houseboats are classified by Kerala's Department of Tourism (not the DTPC) under a Silver / Gold / Diamond tier system, which has superseded the earlier Green Palm Certificate; there is no official "Green Leaf" classification. Silver, Gold, and Diamond boats are required to carry on-board bio-treatment and waste holding tanks; unclassified boats have no such obligation. For peak-season travel (December–January), book quality houseboats 4–6 weeks ahead; for the Christmas–New Year window specifically, allow 8–10 weeks.

⚠ Catches — reconfirm before quoting
  • Budget kettuvallams dump grey and black water directly into the lake; always book a Kerala Tourism Department-classified boat (Silver, Gold, or Diamond tier — the older Green Palm Certificate has been superseded, and any operator using the label 'Green Leaf' should be questioned, as no such official classification exists). Classified boats must carry on-board bio-treatment and waste holding tanks; the price premium over unclassified boats is real, though a figure of ₹2,000–4,000 per night is indicative only and may be out of date — reconfirm before quoting clients.
  • The Nehru Trophy Boat Race is traditionally held on the second Saturday of August but is subject to postponement (the 2024 race was moved to 28 September due to the Wayanad landslides). On race day, Punnamada Lake is cleared of all water traffic, boating from the DTPC jetty to the lake is suspended from 10 AM, and Alleppey town roads face restrictions from 6 AM to 7 PM; houseboat prices spike sharply around the event. Avoid the period unless clients are specifically attending; if they are, book accommodations 3–4 months ahead.
  • Houseboat and resort rates rise 30–50% above rack rates from 24 December to 5 January; routing clients to mid-November or early October delivers comparable conditions at standard prices.
  • Water hyacinth and monsoon debris (June–September) physically block narrow canals, clog propellers, and can strand boats or cut off villages; operators routinely cannot guarantee itinerary routes during this period.
  • At Alleppey jetty, touts redirect arriving travellers to unregistered boats by claiming their booked vessel has been cancelled; always instruct clients to call the operator directly to confirm before disembarking.
  • At Padmanabhaswamy Temple (Trivandrum) and Guruvayur, full-length trousers are explicitly barred for men — dhoti/mundu is mandatory, and some temples additionally require men to be bare-chested; dhotis can be rented or purchased at temple entrances, but clients should confirm availability before visiting.

Why go / vibe

Munnar's rolling carpets of tea and the shola-grassland mosaic of the Western Ghats deliver a cooler, greener alter-ego to coastal Kerala; the combination of wildlife (Nilgiri Tahr, elephants, 55 endemic butterfly species) and genuine plantation heritage makes it more than a pretty backdrop.

Top sights & places within

  • Eravikulam National Park (Rajamalai) — only wild habitat of the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, dramatic open ridgeline at 2,100 m
  • Top Station — highest point on the Kerala–Tamil Nadu border ridge, sweeping valley panorama
  • Kolukkumalai Tea Estate — world's highest organic tea garden (2,100 m), vintage roller-and-dryer factory still in use
  • Mattupetty Dam and Lake — colonial-era shola reservoir with regular elephant sightings at the water's edge
  • Tea Museum, Nallathanni — KDHP estate heritage walk covering 150 years of Munnar plantation history
  • Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary — dry-deciduous fringe on the rain-shadow side, star tortoise and endemic butterflies

Activities menu

  • Tea factory tour and guided estate walk with sit-down Nilgiri leaf tasting
  • Nilgiri Tahr spotting trek inside Eravikulam NP on the Rajamalai trail
  • Anamudi summit trek to 2,695 m — highest peak in South India outside the northeast (Forest Dept permit, strenuous, full day)
  • Coracle or rowboat on Mattupetty Lake with birding on the banks
  • Paragliding at Bison Valley (Oct–Mar, tandem, ~30 min flight over estate canopy)
  • Night wildlife drive at Chinnar — Indian giant squirrel, gaur, endemic spiders

  • Thekkady / Periyar (90 km, 3 h) — Periyar lake boat safari, spice plantation tour, elephant encounter options

What to pack

Layer up (fleece + waterproof shell for evenings year-round), trekking shoes with ankle support, leech socks + salt sachets for trails Oct–May, high-SPF sun protection (UV index severe at altitude), DEET repellent for Chinnar.

Getting there

  • Air: Cochin International Airport (COK) is the standard gateway — 135 km / 4.5–5 h by road via NH183 through Adimali; Madurai Airport (IXM) is 150 km / 4 h from the Tamil Nadu side and useful for circuit itineraries.
  • Rail: No railway in Munnar; nearest stations are Ernakulam Junction (Kochi, 130 km) and Aluva (125 km); pre-booked private taxi ₹2,500–3,500 to Munnar, or shared taxi from Aluva ₹400/seat.
  • Road: From Kochi 135 km (NH183 via Adimali, 4.5 h); from Coimbatore 150 km / 4 h via Udumalpet; KSRTC buses from KSRTC Bus Stand Ernakulam run 3–4 times daily but take 6+ h.

Permits / visa

Indian nationals require no restricted-area or inner-line permit anywhere in Kerala, including Munnar. Eravikulam National Park charges ₹200/adult entry; booking via the official site (eravikulamnationalpark.in) adds a ₹50/person reservation surcharge, and the mandatory shuttle bus to the park core is included in that ₹200 with no separate shuttle fee. The park closes annually Feb 1–Mar 31 for Nilgiri Tahr calving and reopens April 1; Kerala Forest Dept issues exact dates each year, so reconfirm before quoting to clients. The Anamudi summit trek requires a Forest Dept permit obtained at Rajamalai checkpost (early morning); a ₹100/person fee and 25-trekker daily cap are widely cited but could not be confirmed from any official source — reconfirm current terms with the Wildlife Warden's Office Munnar (04865 231587) before quoting. Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary accepts online bookings via booking.munnarwildlife.com, with jeep-safari slots filling quickly on weekends.

Permits / visa

Indian nationals need no visa. Since 2021, the old free paper Inner Line Permit has been abolished and replaced by the Environment and Development Fee (EDF), which costs roughly ₹590 per person for a 7-day trip (₹400 one-time environment fee + ₹20/day wildlife fee + ₹50 Red Cross). The EDF is mandatory for Nubra Valley, Pangong Tso, Tso Moriri, Dah-Hanu tribal belt, Khardung La road, and Turtuk, but is not required for Leh city, Thiksey, Hemis, Alchi, or Shanti Stupa. Apply online at lahdclehpermit.in or in person at DC Office Leh Tourist Section (Mon–Sat); both routes are same-day; carry 5–6 printed photocopies per person as checkpoints at South Pullu/Khardung La, Durbuk, and Pangong entry each collect one copy. The Manali–Leh road typically closes mid-to-late November and reopens late April to late May (BRO-dependent; in 2026 it opened May 12); Zoji La historically closes December through March/April, though BRO kept it open through the entire 2025–26 winter for the first time — the 13.15 km Zoji La Tunnel (excavation breakthrough June 2026, public opening targeted February 2028) will eventually eliminate seasonal closures on that route; reconfirm winter status before quoting. Indian nationals with a valid EDF may access Man and Merak villages beyond Spangmik on the Pangong south bank; foreign nationals are barred past Spangmik; all visitors must avoid photographing military infrastructure, bunkers, or forward posts in the eastern Pangong sector, and drone flights anywhere in Ladakh require prior written permission from the District Magistrate.

Permits / visa

Meghalaya is the wettest corner of India repackaged as its most lush — ancient Khasi and Jaintia tribal culture, a living bioengineering tradition (root bridges grown over centuries), and rivers whose clarity stops visitors mid-sentence. Permits / visa

Indian nationals require no Inner Line Permit (ILP) or Restricted Area Permit (RAP) for Meghalaya; a valid Aadhaar or voter ID suffices. Meghalaya is actively pursuing an ILP-style entry permit under the Immigration and Foreigners Act, 2025, and the Union government has responded positively — if enacted, both Indian and foreign nationals would need a permit to enter the state, so reconfirm status with Meghalaya Tourism before booking. Foreign nationals on short-stay tourist visas (under 180 days) are not required to register with the FRRO; that obligation applies only to Pakistani nationals and long-stay visa holders, and where required, registration is completed via the e-FRRO online portal (indianfrro.gov.in) — an in-person visit to East Khasi Hills FRO, Nongrim Hills, Shillong is only required when specifically directed by the authority. No RAP is required for mainstream tourist areas, but Dawki, Baghmara, and Balpakram are border-adjacent zones that may fall under Protected Area or Restricted Area rules for foreign nationals; verify with the Meghalaya Tourism office in Shillong before booking those stops. Citizens of Pakistan, China, Afghanistan, and Myanmar face additional entry scrutiny. Forest entry fees are ₹50–200 per person at Sacred Grove Mawphlang, Mawsmai Cave, and state eco-parks.
