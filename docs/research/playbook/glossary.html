Glossary · Driftibo Operator Playbook

Glossary — every short-form, in plain words

If a term in the playbook lost you, it's here in plain English — and why it matters to you as the agent.

This page spells out every short-form used in the playbook; tap or click a term in the list, or use the A–Z bar below to jump straight to a letter.

A B C D E F G I K L M N O P S T V

A

ADTOI (Association of Domestic Tour Operators of India)

The trade body that represents tour operators focused on travel within India.

Why it matters to you: Membership signals credibility to hotels and suppliers and can open doors to industry events and preferential rate negotiations.

Air-charter broker (middleman who arranges whole or partial aircraft hire)

An intermediary who connects clients needing a chartered flight with operators that own aircraft, without owning any planes themselves.

Why it matters to you: When a high-value client wants a private or group charter, the broker is your point of contact — you do not approach the aircraft operator directly.

ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)

The predictable revenue a business earns on a repeating annual basis; distinct from GMV (Gross Merchandise Value), which is the total transaction value flowing through the business before costs or commissions are deducted.

Why it matters to you: Investors and accelerators look at ARR as a stability metric, but tracking GMV alongside it shows the total bookings volume your agency is handling.

ATOAI (Adventure Tour Operators Association of India)

The industry body representing operators who offer adventure travel products — trekking, rafting, mountaineering, and similar activities — within India.

Why it matters to you: If you intend to sell adventure packages, ATOAI membership lends credibility with suppliers and demonstrates to clients that you operate to recognised safety and quality standards.

B

B2B portal (Business-to-Business portal)

An online platform that suppliers — hotels, airlines, transfer companies — make available exclusively to trade partners such as travel agents, not to the general public.

Why it matters to you: B2B portals typically show net rates lower than public prices, which is where your margin comes from.

BCAS (Bureau of Civil Aviation Security)

The Indian government body under the Ministry of Civil Aviation responsible for setting and enforcing security standards at airports and for all civil aviation operations, including charter and helicopter services.

Why it matters to you: When you arrange helicopter or charter bookings, the operator you work with must comply with BCAS regulations; knowing this helps you ask the right due-diligence questions before committing a client booking.

Bedbank (wholesale hotel-room bank / B2B hotel wholesaler)

A company that buys hotel room inventory in bulk from properties worldwide and resells it at net rates to travel agents and tour operators.

Why it matters to you: Accessing a bedbank lets you offer hotel options far beyond what you could negotiate directly, often at competitive wholesale prices.

BSP (Billing and Settlement Plan)

IATA's multilateral payment and reporting system that consolidates the money flows between airlines and accredited travel agents into a single, regular settlement cycle.

Why it matters to you: If you hold or plan to hold an IATA appointment, your airline ticket sales and the commissions or net payments due to you will flow through BSP — understanding it helps you manage cash flow and reconcile your accounts accurately.

C

CA (Chartered Accountant)

A qualified accounting professional licensed by the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI) to perform audits, prepare financial statements, and advise on taxation.

Why it matters to you: Your CA handles GST registrations and filings, TDS/TCS compliance, annual accounts, and income tax returns — choosing one with travel-trade experience from day one saves costly corrections later.

CCPA (Central Consumer Protection Authority)

The statutory authority established under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, empowered to investigate unfair trade practices, misleading advertisements, and violations of consumer rights, and to issue fines or recall orders.

Why it matters to you: Travel agencies fall within CCPA's jurisdiction; having clear, honest terms and conditions and a documented refund policy is your best protection against a complaint or investigation.

CGST / SGST / IGST (Central GST / State GST / Integrated GST)

The three components of India's GST system. CGST and SGST are levied together on supplies made within a single state — the Centre and State each take half. IGST is levied instead on supplies that cross a state border or involve imports, and is collected entirely by the Centre before being apportioned.

Why it matters to you: The component that applies to each invoice depends on where you and your client are located; using the wrong one creates reconciliation problems and demands a credit note, so your invoicing software must be configured correctly from the start.

Consolidator (airfare wholesaler that buys airline seats in bulk)

A company that purchases large blocks of airline tickets from carriers at discounted rates and resells them to travel agents at prices below the publicly available fare.

Why it matters to you: Booking through a consolidator can help you offer clients lower airfares while still earning a margin.

D

DMC (Destination Management Company)

A local specialist company at a destination that designs and executes ground arrangements — tours, venues, transport, guides — on behalf of agents and operators based elsewhere.

Why it matters to you: A reliable DMC lets you sell complex itineraries in places you have never visited without carrying the on-ground operational risk yourself.

E

e-visa (electronic visa applied for online before travel)

A visa approval that is processed and issued digitally through a government website, allowing the traveller to arrive without needing a physical stamp or sticker in the passport beforehand.

Why it matters to you: Helping clients obtain e-visas is a low-effort, high-trust add-on that can command a handling fee and deepens your relationship as a one-stop shop.

Empty-leg (discounted one-way repositioning charter flight)

A private or charter flight that must fly empty to reposition an aircraft after dropping off passengers, sold at a deep discount to fill the otherwise revenue-less segment.

Why it matters to you: Empty-legs can be a compelling deal for flexible premium clients, and brokering them earns commission with minimal extra effort.

F

FIT / GIT (Free/Foreign Independent Traveller / Group Inclusive Tour)

FIT refers to a traveller or small party who books a custom, self-directed itinerary; GIT refers to a pre-packaged tour sold to a group travelling together under a single booking.

Why it matters to you: FIT clients demand more personalisation and earn higher per-person margins, while GIT business drives volume — your pricing model differs significantly for each.

FOREX / LRS (Foreign Exchange / Liberalised Remittance Scheme)

FOREX is simply foreign currency; LRS is the framework under which residents can remit foreign currency abroad up to a permitted annual ceiling for approved purposes such as travel and education.

Why it matters to you: When clients pay for overseas travel, the transaction may trigger LRS compliance obligations and TCS deductions that you need to communicate clearly.

Form 143 (declaration form referenced for travel payments)

A form used in certain travel-payment contexts to declare the purpose and nature of a transaction as required by the relevant authority or bank.

Why it matters to you: Knowing when this form is required prevents payment delays and keeps your documentation in order during compliance checks.

Form A2 (bank form for an outward foreign-currency payment under FEMA)

The standard declaration form that banks require when a resident makes an outward remittance in foreign currency, confirming the purpose falls within permitted categories under the Foreign Exchange Management Act.

Why it matters to you: Your clients or your own business may need to complete Form A2 when paying overseas suppliers, and familiarity with it speeds up the payment process considerably.

G

GDS (Global Distribution System)

A centralised platform that aggregates real-time availability and pricing for flights, hotels, and car rentals from thousands of suppliers worldwide, letting agents search and book them in one place.

Why it matters to you: GDS access gives you a professional booking tool and qualified fares, though it comes with training and subscription costs that smaller agencies weigh carefully.

GMV (Gross Merchandise Value)

The total value of all bookings transacted through your business in a given period, before deducting supplier costs, commissions paid out, or any other expenses.

Why it matters to you: GMV shows how much travel you are selling, but your actual revenue is only the margin you retain on top of that figure — the two numbers tell very different stories.

Ground handler (local operator running transport, transfers and logistics on the ground)

A company at the destination that manages physical logistics — airport transfers, coaches, guides, porterage — for groups or individuals once they arrive in-country.

Why it matters to you: A reliable ground handler is the difference between a smooth client experience and a logistical disaster; their performance reflects directly on your agency's reputation.

GST (Goods and Services Tax)

The indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services in India, collected at various stages of the supply chain.

Why it matters to you: Travel services attract GST at rates that vary by service type, and you must charge, collect, and file it correctly to stay compliant and avoid penalties.

GSTIN (Goods and Services Tax Identification Number)

Your unique 15-character GST registration number, derived from your PAN and state code, which identifies your business in the GST system and must appear on every tax invoice you raise.

Why it matters to you: Corporate clients need your GSTIN to claim Input Tax Credit on the GST they pay you; without it on your invoice, they will reject the document and delay payment.

I

IATA (International Air Transport Association)

The global trade association for airlines that also accredits travel agencies, enabling them to issue airline tickets directly and access standard commission or net fares.

Why it matters to you: IATA accreditation is a widely recognised credential that gives you direct ticketing access and signals professionalism to corporate clients.

IATO (Indian Association of Tour Operators)

The apex industry body representing tour operators in India, focused on promoting inbound, outbound, and domestic tourism and providing advocacy with government bodies.

Why it matters to you: IATO membership can open access to ministry-level representation, industry networking, and enhanced credibility with international partners sending clients to India.

ILP (Inner Line Permit)

A travel document issued by the state government that Indian citizens require to visit designated restricted border areas in states such as Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Mizoram, and Manipur; foreign nationals require a separate Protected Area Permit for some of the same zones.

Why it matters to you: Clients travelling to these regions without a valid ILP will be turned back at check-points; arranging it in advance — or clearly instructing clients how to obtain it — is an essential part of your itinerary preparation.

IRCTC (Indian Railway Catering and Tourism Corporation)

The government-of-India undertaking under the Ministry of Railways that manages online rail ticketing, catering, and tourism packages; it is also the sole authorised body to sell per-seat helicopter shuttle tickets for the Char Dham circuit.

Why it matters to you: For Char Dham helicopter-seat bookings, IRCTC is the only legitimate channel — directing clients anywhere else exposes them to fraud and you to liability.

IRDAI (Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India)

The statutory body that regulates the insurance sector in India, licensing insurers and intermediaries, approving products, and protecting policyholders.

Why it matters to you: Any travel insurance product you recommend or sell must be issued by an IRDAI-licensed insurer; selling or facilitating unlicensed insurance products is a regulatory offence.

ITC (Input Tax Credit)

The mechanism under GST that allows a registered business to offset the tax it paid on its own purchases and expenses against the GST it collects from clients, so that only the net value-added is taxed.

Why it matters to you: Correctly claiming ITC on your eligible business expenses — office rent, software subscriptions, certain services — reduces the net GST you owe each filing period; your CA can identify what qualifies.

K

KYC (Know Your Customer)

The identity and address verification process that regulated entities — banks, payment gateways, money changers — are required by law to complete before providing financial services to a customer.

Why it matters to you: Your payment gateway and forex partners will require KYC documents from you and, in some cases, from your clients; having a standard document checklist ready speeds up onboarding and prevents transaction holds.

L

LLP (Limited Liability Partnership)

A business structure registered under the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008, in which partners' personal liability is limited to their agreed capital contribution, while the entity itself has a separate legal identity.

Why it matters to you: An LLP is a popular and relatively affordable structure for small travel agencies — it gives you limited liability protection and is easier to maintain than a private limited company, while still being accepted by most corporate clients and banks.

M

Markup (the amount the agent adds on top of the net rate)

The difference between the net (wholesale) price you pay a supplier and the selling price you charge the client — this is the primary way you earn revenue on a booking.

Why it matters to you: Getting your markup strategy right — neither too thin to be unsustainable nor too high to lose clients to competitors — is the core of your pricing discipline.

MICE (Meetings, Incentives, Conferences and Exhibitions)

A segment of the travel industry that handles group business travel for corporate events — conferences, award trips, trade shows, and team retreats — rather than leisure travellers.

Why it matters to you: MICE clients book large groups and pay premium rates, making them high-value contracts, but they also demand detailed logistics and tight coordination.

MoT (Ministry of Tourism, Government of India)

The central government ministry that sets national tourism policy, administers the Incredible India brand, runs the Tourist Facilitator certification programme, and distributes grants and recognition to approved tour operators.

Why it matters to you: MoT recognition or certification can be a differentiator when pitching to international inbound partners and government-linked travel programmes; it is also the body whose approval is required for certain subsidised tourism schemes.

N

NDC (New Distribution Capability)

An IATA-defined data standard that allows airlines to distribute rich, personalised offers — including ancillaries like seat upgrades and baggage — directly to agents and booking platforms, bypassing the traditional GDS model.

Why it matters to you: As more airlines shift to NDC, you may need NDC-enabled platform access to see the best fares and bundling options available to your clients.

Net rate (the wholesale price before the agent's markup)

The confidential price that a supplier quotes to a trade partner, on the understanding that the agent will add a markup before presenting a final price to the client.

Why it matters to you: Net rates are the foundation of your margin — protect them carefully, and never expose them to clients.

NOC (No Objection Certificate)

A document issued by an authority — a landlord, municipality, government department, or registered society — formally stating that they have no objection to a specified activity, transaction, or appointment.

Why it matters to you: You may encounter NOC requirements when registering your business address, applying for certain licences, or when clients need documentation for adventure activities or restricted-area permits; knowing to ask for one early prevents last-minute delays.

NSOP (Non-Scheduled Operator Permit)

The permit issued by the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) authorising a company to operate charter (non-scheduled) air transport services in India, including helicopter and private aircraft charters.

Why it matters to you: Any helicopter or charter operator you work with should hold a valid NSOP; verifying this before confirming a booking protects your clients and shields you from liability if an unlicensed operator is involved in an incident.

O

Operator (charter) (the company that actually owns and flies the aircraft)

The aviation company that holds the air operator certificate, owns or leases the aircraft, and is legally responsible for the flight — distinct from a broker who merely arranges the booking.

Why it matters to you: When selling a charter, knowing whether you are dealing with the operator directly or through a broker affects your pricing, liability, and contract terms.

P

PAN (Permanent Account Number)

A unique ten-character alphanumeric identifier issued by the Indian Income Tax Department to individuals and entities for tracking financial transactions and tax records.

Why it matters to you: You will need your business PAN for GST registration, TCS and TDS filings, and any significant financial transactions with suppliers or clients.

PNR (Passenger Name Record)

A unique booking reference code in a GDS or airline system that stores all the details of a passenger's itinerary — flights, seat assignments, contact information, and special requests.

Why it matters to you: The PNR is your paper trail for every airline booking; you need it to make changes, check in clients, or resolve service issues with the carrier.

POSP (Point of Salesperson — IRDAI insurance category)

An IRDAI-defined category of individual authorised to sell a limited range of straightforward insurance products — such as basic travel insurance — after completing a short mandatory training and certification, without needing a full insurance broker or agent licence.

Why it matters to you: Registering as a POSP through a licensed insurer or insurance marketing firm is a low-barrier way to legitimately earn a commission on travel insurance policies you recommend to clients.

S

SAC (Service Accounting Code)

The six-digit classification code defined by the GST Council to categorise each type of service for GST purposes, analogous to HSN codes used for goods; it determines the applicable GST rate and the correct reporting head on your returns.

Why it matters to you: Every service you invoice — tour packages, visa handling, hotel bookings — must carry the correct SAC; an incorrect code attracts the wrong tax rate and can trigger demand notices during a GST audit.

T

TAAI (Travel Agents Association of India)

One of India's established trade associations for travel agents, providing advocacy, training, and networking for its members across the country.

Why it matters to you: TAAI membership lends credibility to your agency and connects you to a peer network and supplier community that can support your growth.

TAN (Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number)

A unique ten-character alphanumeric number issued by the Income Tax Department that an entity must obtain before it is legally permitted to deduct TDS or collect TCS on behalf of the government.

Why it matters to you: Once your turnover or transaction profile triggers a TDS or TCS obligation, you need a TAN to file the returns and deposit the deducted/collected tax — operating without one when required attracts penalties.

TCS (Tax Collected at Source)

A tax that the seller collects from the buyer at the point of sale on specified transaction types — in travel, this commonly applies to overseas tour packages above a threshold — and then remits to the government.

Why it matters to you: You may be required to collect TCS from clients on qualifying transactions and deposit it with tax authorities, so your invoicing system must handle this correctly.

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)

A tax deducted by the payer before making a payment to a supplier or contractor, remitted directly to the government on the payee's behalf.

Why it matters to you: If corporate clients or accounts deduct TDS on payments to you, you must account for it in your tax filings to avoid being taxed twice on the same income.

TIDS (Travel Industry Designator Service)

An IATA programme that assigns a unique numeric code to travel agencies that are not IATA-accredited, enabling them to be formally identified by airlines and other travel suppliers when making bookings or claiming commission.

Why it matters to you: If you are not yet IATA-accredited, obtaining a TIDS code is a practical first step toward being recognised by airlines and suppliers as a legitimate trade partner.

V

Visa-as-a-service (offering visa-application handling as a paid add-on)

A service model in which the agent or a specialist partner manages the entire visa application process for the client — gathering documents, filing forms, tracking status — in exchange for a service fee.

Why it matters to you: It is a low-capital revenue stream that adds genuine convenience and keeps clients engaged with your agency beyond the core flight and hotel booking.

VOA (Visa On Arrival)

A facility offered by some countries that allows eligible passport holders to obtain their visa at the port of entry rather than applying in advance from home.

Why it matters to you: Knowing which destinations offer VOA to your clients' nationalities removes a common booking barrier and lets you pitch those destinations more confidently.

Voucher (the confirmation document that proves a booking is paid or held)

A document issued by the agent or supplier to the client confirming that a specific service — hotel room, transfer, tour — has been booked and either paid for or guaranteed.

Why it matters to you: Vouchers are your client's proof of booking at the destination and your protection if a supplier disputes whether a service was ever arranged.
